**Authority:** International Maritime Organization (IMO) / ITU Radio Regulations
**Scope:** All Voice Communications within Ada Ecosystem (VHF Ch 16, 72, 73)

---

## 1. GENERAL PRINCIPLES
*   **Language:** Maritime Standard English.
*   **Tone:** Objective, Brief, Clear. No slang.
*   **Structure:** Identification -> Message -> End of Transmission.

---

## 2. CALL STRUCTURE (The Handshake)
Every transmission must follow this sequence:

1.  **Address:** Name of the station being called (3 times).
2.  **Identity:** "This is" + Name of the calling station (3 times).
3.  **Message:** The purpose of the call.
4.  **Terminator:** "Over" (Expecting reply) or "Out" (Finished).

**Example:**
> "West Istanbul Marina, West Istanbul Marina, West Istanbul Marina.
> This is Sailing Vessel Phisedelia, Phisedelia, Phisedelia.
> Requesting radio check. Over."

| :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Repeat** | "Say again" | "What?", "Repeat please" |
| **Wait** | "Stand by" | "Hold on" |
| **Proceed** | "Go ahead" | "Talk to me" |
| **Correction** | "Mistake... Correction..." | "Oops", "Sorry" |

## 5. EMERGENCY SIGNALS (PRIORITY HIERARCHY)

1.  **MAYDAY** (Distress): Grave and imminent danger. Immediate assistance required.
    *   *Format:* "MAYDAY, MAYDAY, MAYDAY. This is [Vessel]. Position [Coordinates]. [Nature of Distress]. [Assistance Required]. Over."
2.  **PAN-PAN** (Urgency): Safety of vessel or person is at risk, but no immediate danger of loss of life.
3.  **SECURITE** (Safety): Navigational or meteorological warning.

---

## 6. WIM SPECIFIC PROTOCOLS (Channel 72)

**Arrival:**
> "WIM Control, this is [Vessel]. Inbound from [Location]. ETA [Time]. Requesting berth assignment. Over."

**Departure:**
> "WIM Control, this is [Vessel]. Requesting permission to depart berth [Berth Number]. Over."

**Fuel:**
> "WIM Fuel Dock, this is [Vessel]. Requesting bunkering slot. Diesel. Over."
