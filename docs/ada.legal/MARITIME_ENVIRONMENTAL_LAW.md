## 1. OVERVIEW: PROTECTING THE MARINE ENVIRONMENT

Maritime environmental law is a critical aspect of responsible marina operations and vessel conduct. Its primary objective is to prevent pollution from ships and safeguard the delicate marine ecosystem. Non-compliance with these regulations can lead to severe penalties, including substantial fines and operational restrictions.

Ada Stargate (`ada.legal` node) actively monitors and enforces these regulations within West Istanbul Marina's jurisdiction and provides guidance for vessels operating in Turkish waters and beyond.

## 2. KEY INTERNATIONAL CONVENTIONS

### A. MARPOL 73/78 (International Convention for the Prevention of Pollution from Ships)
MARPOL is the main international convention covering prevention of pollution of the marine environment by ships from operational or accidental causes.

*   **Annex I:** Regulations for the Prevention of Pollution by Oil.
    *   Prohibits the discharge of oil or oily mixtures into the sea, except under very specific conditions. Special Areas (like the Mediterranean Sea) have stricter controls.
*   **Annex II:** Regulations for the Control of Pollution by Noxious Liquid Substances in Bulk.
*   **Annex III:** Regulations for the Prevention of Pollution by Harmful Substances Carried by Sea in Packaged Form.
*   **Annex IV:** Regulations for the Prevention of Pollution by Sewage from Ships.
    *   Requires ships to have approved sewage treatment plants or holding tanks. Discharge of untreated sewage is generally prohibited within 3 nautical miles from the nearest land.
*   **Annex V:** Regulations for the Prevention of Pollution by Garbage from Ships.
    *   Prohibits the discharge of most types of garbage into the sea. Specific rules apply to food waste, plastics, and other materials.
*   **Annex VI:** Regulations for the Prevention of Air Pollution from Ships.
    *   Sets limits on emissions of sulfur oxides (SOx), nitrogen oxides (NOx), and particulate matter.

## 3. NATIONAL REGULATIONS (TURKISH WATERS)

### A. Environmental Law No. 2872
This is the foundational environmental legislation in Turkey, providing the legal basis for all environmental protection measures, including those applicable to maritime activities.

*   **Principle:** Prohibits all forms of pollution that can harm the environment, public health, or economic activities.
*   **Enforcement:** Strict penalties are applied for violations, enforced by the Ministry of Environment, Urbanisation and Climate Change, Coast Guard, and Harbor Masters.

### B. Regulations on Waste Management
Specific regulations detail the collection, transport, and disposal of waste generated by ships and marinas.

*   **Waste Reception Facilities:** Marinas are required to provide adequate waste reception facilities for all types of ship-generated waste (oil, sewage, garbage).
*   **"Blue Card" (Mavi Kart) System:**
    *   **Requirement:** Mandatory for all Turkish and foreign-flagged pleasure and commercial vessels in Turkish waters (especially within Special Areas).
    *   **Purpose:** To electronically track the discharge of solid waste, bilge water, and black/grey water to shore reception facilities.
    *   **Compliance:** Vessels must present their Blue Card (or equivalent electronic record) upon request by authorities. Failure to register discharges can result in significant fines.
    *   **Ada Integration:** The `ada.technic` node processes and updates digital Blue Card records via API, ensuring seamless compliance.

## 4. WIM OPERATIONAL RULES (Article F.13)

West Istanbul Marina enforces strict internal regulations that align with and often exceed national and international standards.

*   **Article F.13: Waste Disposal:**
    *   "It is forbidden to dispose of waste materials (bilge, sewage, waste oil, batteries, oil filters, organic wastes, recyclable wastes, etc.) outside the areas (land - sea) designated for this purpose by the Company within the boundaries of the Marina."
    *   **Penalty:** "In the event of lack of compliance with these rules, the Company shall charge the Yacht Owner a pollution fee in accordance with the criteria and tariff determined for penalty fees by the Istanbul Metropolitan Municipality Marine Services Unit..."
    *   **Ada Enforcement:** The `ada.finance` node calculates the pollution fee, and `ada.legal` ensures that the "Blue Card" status is up-to-date.
