## 3. BEAUFORT WIND SCALE
Standard scale for describing wind intensity based on sea state.

| Force | Description | Speed (knots) | Sea State |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0** | Calm | < 1 | Sea like a mirror. |
| **1** | Light Air | 1-3 | Ripples with the appearance of scales are formed. |
| **2** | Light Breeze | 4-6 | Small wavelets, still short, but more pronounced. |
| **3** | Gentle Breeze | 7-10 | Large wavelets. Crests begin to break. Foam of glassy appearance. |
| **4** | Moderate Breeze | 11-16 | Small waves, becoming longer; fairly frequent white horses. |
| **5** | Fresh Breeze | 17-21 | Moderate waves, taking a more pronounced long form; many white horses. |
| **6** | Strong Breeze | 22-27 | Large waves begin to form; the white foam crests are more extensive. |
| **7** | Near Gale | 28-33 | Sea heaps up and white foam from breaking waves begins to be blown in streaks. |
| **8** | Gale | 34-40 | Moderately high waves of greater length; edges of crests begin to break into spindrift. |
| **9** | Strong Gale | 41-47 | High waves. Dense streaks of foam along the direction of the wind. Spray may affect visibility. |
| **10** | Storm | 48-55 | Very high waves with long overhanging crests. The resulting foam, in great patches, is blown in dense white streaks. |

## 4. STANDARD MARITIME GLOSSARY

*   **Aft:** Toward the back of the boat.
*   **Beam:** The widest point of the boat.
*   **Bow:** The front of the boat.
*   **Draft:** The depth of water required to float the vessel.
*   **Fathom:** A unit of length equal to 6 feet (1.8 meters), used for depth.
*   **Freeboard:** Distance from the waterline to the lowest point of the deck.
*   **Heel:** The tilt of a boat to one side.
*   **Helm:** The steering wheel or tiller.
*   **Leeward:** The direction away from the wind (Downwind).
*   **Port:** The left side of the boat when facing forward (Red light).
*   **Starboard:** The right side of the boat when facing forward (Green light).
*   **Windward:** The direction from which the wind is blowing (Upwind).
